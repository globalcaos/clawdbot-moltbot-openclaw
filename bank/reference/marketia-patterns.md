# Marketia — Marketing Copy Patterns

## Sales Funnel (applies to ALL skill descriptions)

1. **HOOK** — emotional, snappy, makes them feel something
2. **SELL** — what it does, why it's different, why they need it
3. **CONVERT** — gently guide to GitHub fork: https://github.com/globalcaos/clawdbot-moltbot-openclaw

## Signature Lines (Oscar-approved)

- _"Research papers included, because we're that kind of obsessive."_
- _"Clone it. Fork it. Break it. Make it yours."_ — embodies TheTinkerZone tinkering spirit

## Rules

- ONE option per section. No A/B. Commit to the best.
- No buzzwords: "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", etc.
- Short description: max 120 chars
- Always link to ai-humor-ultimate pairing where relevant
- Fork link lands AFTER visitor is already invested — natural conversion, not hard sell

## Tone

- Snappy, emotional, differentiated
- Can reference pop culture (Iron Man, etc.) when natural
- Humor welcome — but it has to land
- Audience: technical OpenClaw users who want their agent to feel alive

## Lessons Learned (accumulated from iterations)

### WhatsApp Ultimate

- "Won't" repetition pattern works brilliantly — each one is a real fear, delivered with a grin
- Frame benefits as what you PREVENT, not what you provide
- "It replied to your mom" → too ambiguous, sounded like OUR product failed. Fixed by flipping to humor.
- Pain should come from OTHER tools / unguarded agents, never from ours

### Outlook Hack

- "Just share the tab" sounded like the lame extension approach. Fixed by emphasizing: share ONCE, skill captures tokens, then autonomous all day
- "One handshake in the morning" = keeper line
- Code-enforced safety (can't send) is more powerful than policy-based safety (won't send)
- "Chainsaw from a toddler — completely and without negotiation" = top-tier line
- "It writes drafts — you pull the trigger. Sleep well." = perfect closer
- The 3 dead ends (API/IMAP/extension) work as quick pain setup, but don't over-explain — summarize with humor

### CRITICAL RULE (learned 3x — never break this)

- NEVER open with pain that sounds like OUR product failing
- "Your agent asks what project" = sounds like OUR agent forgot. NO.
- Correct structure: VALUE FIRST → pain without us (brief) → how we do it
- Lead with strength. Pain is background context, not the hook.
- Our product WORKS. Other products fail. Make that crystal clear.

### General Evolution

- v1: marketing blurb (useless)
- v2: functional but dry
- v3: humor + pain/gain + conversion funnel = winning formula
- Each iteration: client refines the nuance, Marketia absorbs and improves
- The humor should make people SMILE and UNDERSTAND simultaneously
- Every "won't" line should be a real scenario the reader has experienced or feared
